# Design: Delhi Excise Policy Case Interactive Explainer

**Date:** 2026-02-28
**Status:** Approved

## Overview

An interactive, long-form web explainer about the Delhi Excise Policy case (CBI vs Kuldeep Singh & Others), styled after Paras Chopra's explainers. The central theme is the legal distinction between "discharge" and "acquittal" — addressing the political narrative around the February 27, 2026 order.

## Key Facts

- **Court:** Special Court (PC Act), Rouse Avenue, New Delhi — NOT the Supreme Court
- **Judge:** Sh. Jitendra Singh
- **Order:** All 23 accused discharged (Order on Charge, not acquittal after trial)
- **Date:** 27.02.2026
- **Key accused:** Arvind Kejriwal (A-18), Manish Sisodia (A-8), K. Kavitha (A-17), and 20 others

## Narrative Structure

1. **What Just Happened** — Opening hook, plain language
2. **Discharge vs Acquittal** — Core legal distinction (centerpiece)
3. **The Allegation** — CBI's theory in plain language
4. **The Cast** — All 23 accused and their alleged roles
5. **What the Court Found** — Systematic analysis of each allegation pillar
6. **The Verdict** — Key quotes from the order
7. **Issues the Court Flagged** — Fair investigation concerns, PMLA, election domain
8. **What This Means** — Neutral summary of implications

## Interactive Elements

- Criminal case stage slider (FIR → Investigation → Charge → Trial → Verdict)
- Event timeline (Oct 2020 – Feb 2026)
- Accused network grid (click to explore)
- Quote cards with paragraph references
- Discharge vs Acquittal comparison table

## Visual Style

- Georgia serif, cream background (#faf9f7), 680px content width
- Indigo accents for interactive elements
- Sidenotes for legal definitions
- Mobile responsive

## Technical

- Single standalone index.html
- No build tools, no frameworks
- Pure HTML/CSS/JS
- ~3000-4000 lines

## Tone

- Objective, neutral — no sides taken
- Plain language — accessible to non-lawyers
- Let the court's own words speak
